Privacy Policy

Last updated: {{ policy_updated or '2026-06-01' }}

This Privacy Policy describes how {{ config.instance_name or 'HomeHub' }} (“the Service”) handles information when you use our family dashboard. The Service is operated by the household that runs this server (“we”, “us”, “operator”). This is a self-hosted application—not a commercial SaaS product.

Who operates the Service

Data is stored on infrastructure controlled by the operator of this HomeHub instance (your family’s server or hosting provider). Contact the operator of this site for questions: {{ operator_contact or 'the household administrator' }}.

Information we collect

  • Account sign-in (Firebase / Google): When authentication is enabled, we receive your Google account identifier, email address, and display name to verify you are on the family allow list and to attribute content you create.
  • Calendar data (optional): If you connect Google Calendar, we store OAuth tokens (encrypted) and calendar event data (titles, dates, times, descriptions) in our local database so the family calendar can display and sync events.
  • Content you add: Reminders, notes, shopping lists, and other features store the text and metadata you submit on this server.
  • Technical logs: The server may log IP addresses, request paths, and errors for security and troubleshooting.

How we use information

  • Authenticate family members and enforce access controls.
  • Display and synchronize shared calendar and household information among authorized users.
  • Operate, secure, and maintain the Service.

We do not sell your personal information.

Third-party services

  • Google / Firebase: Used for sign-in and (if enabled) Google Calendar API access. Google’s policies apply to their processing: Google Privacy Policy.
  • Weather (optional): If enabled, the browser may request forecast data from Open-Meteo; see their terms at open-meteo.com.

Storage and security

Data is stored in a SQLite database and local files on the operator’s server. Google Calendar refresh tokens are encrypted at rest using the server’s secret key. Access is restricted to users on the configured allow list (and calendar sharing rules you configure).

Retention and deletion

Data is kept until the operator deletes it or removes your account from the allow list. You may ask the operator to delete your connected calendar data or disconnect Google Calendar from the dashboard settings. Disconnecting removes stored OAuth tokens; optional cleanup of imported events is controlled by the operator.

Children

The Service is intended for use by members of a single household under the operator’s control. Parents or guardians decide which family accounts are permitted and which calendars are shared.

Changes

The operator may update this policy by editing the deployed template. The “Last updated” date above should be revised when material changes are made.
